Our Commitment

Our Commitment

“We are not going to be served, but to serve.”

  1. 01 Always obey the guidance of the team leaders.
  2. 02 Strive for unity with fellow team members.
  3. 03 Do not leave the mission site or act alone.
  4. 04 Always report to a leader before going anywhere (move in groups of 2–3 or more).
  5. 05 Remember that we are always little Christs.
  6. 06 Put others before myself and encourage one another.
  7. 07 Eat and drink only what is approved at the mission site.
  8. 08 Carry out every assigned ministry with my whole heart.
  9. 09 Give thanks in all things and never complain.
  10. 10 Never lose joy before everyone I meet.

I pledge before God and my team
to keep all of the above on my own.

Prayer Topics

Prayer for Shalom Latin America

  1. May God Himself lead every part and process, so that all preparation and execution unfold in His grace, and every part of the schedule is used preciously within His will.
  2. May the partnering Global Mission Churches of Korea, Virginia, and Washington serve with one heart, and may the pastors who preach the Word be granted wisdom and power.
  3. May the Lord protect the culture ministry and the Sunday Korean-church ministry that follow, so that the whole journey ends safely and in good health.

Prayer for the Shalom Latin America Team

  1. In the ministry shared with the missionaries and their 33 children (MKs), may every heart be opened wide and may it be a time of healing and hope.
  2. May every gathering, praise, and performance give glory to God, and may all who take part experience His comfort and love.
  3. May the hair & beauty, facial skincare, Korean-medicine, and MK teams serve without lack, and may the intercessory team be supporters who continually hold them up in prayer.

Prayer for Missionaries & MKs

  1. May the participating missionaries find their weary bodies and hearts restored through this event, and experience true shalom.
  2. May all of Latin America become an outpost for spreading the gospel through the missionaries on that land.
  3. May the MKs personally meet the living Lord, rediscover their identity and calling as missionary kids, and take root in faith.

Guatemala Guide & Culture

Guatemala

Basic Info

Capital & PopulationGuatemala City · approx. 18 million (one of the most populous countries in Central America)
Language & ReligionSpanish (official) · 22 Mayan languages / Catholic 45%, Protestant 42%
Climate & Time“Land of Eternal Spring” (cool in the highlands) / 2 hours behind US Eastern Time (summer)
Power & Currency110V · US-style plugs (A/B) / Quetzal; USD widely accepted (small bills recommended)

Spiritual Climate & Safety

  • Protestant growth — one of the most evangelical countries in Central America; locals are very familiar with the blessing greeting (Dios le bendiga).
  • Syncretism — in indigenous areas, rituals blending traditional Mayan belief with Catholicism remain, so it is important to respect the culture and share the gospel wisely.
  • Health — no tap water; always bottled (Agua Pura). Avoid street food; peel fruit. Carry hand sanitizer.
  • Altitude — the capital and Antigua are above 1,500 m. Take it easy the first 1–2 days and stay well hydrated.
  • Security — no walking alone at night, keep valuables hidden, always get consent before photographing locals. Follow the leaders’ instructions.

Food Culture

StaplesCorn tortillas, black beans (frijoles), rice, chicken
Traditional DishPepián — a signature thick, spicy stew of meat and vegetables
CoffeeWorld-class coffee region — a rich culture of quality coffee after meals

Local Weather

Weather · Guatemala City

Guatemala City sits at about 1,500 m, with a cool climate often called “eternal spring.” June–October is the rainy season: mostly clear mornings with afternoon-to-evening showers or thunderstorms. Concentrate ministry and tours in the morning, and keep an umbrella and light jacket ready for afternoon rain.

Rainy-season tips   The day-night temperature swing is large, so bring a light jacket for morning and evening and always carry a folding umbrella or poncho. UV is strong, so sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses are essential. (Conditions may vary with local weather.)

Culture Tour · Antigua

Antigua Guatemala

The UNESCO World Heritage city of Antigua Guatemala — a beautiful former capital where Spanish colonial Baroque architecture meets volcano views. On Saturday, July 4, we look together at the marks of God’s creation and history along the journey.

Tour notes   It is high altitude (above 1,500 m), so bring a light jacket and comfortable shoes. Watch for pickpockets, keep valuables hidden, and always move in groups of three under the leaders’ guidance.
